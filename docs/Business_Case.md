# Business Case: Receivables Analytics & Working Capital Optimization

## Executive Summary
A mid-sized Security Services Company providing manpower security solutions to 34 enterprise contractor clients is experiencing severe working capital pressure. Although the company is highly profitable on paper, recurring payment delays of 60–90 days from a subset of clients are causing significant cash flow instability. Security guards must be paid monthly regardless of client invoice settlement, exacerbating the liquidity constraints.

Deloitte's Analytics & Business Transformation practice has been engaged to design an end-to-end receivables intelligence system using SQL and Power BI to provide the CEO, CFO, and Finance Leadership Team with clear visibility into payment behaviors, aging risks, and cash flow forecasts.

## Problem Statement
The Finance Leadership Team lacks real-time visibility into contractor payment behaviors and the aging of accounts receivable. Specifically, management cannot easily identify:
- Which contractors are causing the highest collection delays.
- Which clients are likely to delay future payments based on historical behavior.
- The cash flow gaps expected in the upcoming months.
- The effectiveness and efficiency of current collection efforts.
- Concentration risk (over-reliance on a few contractors with poor payment histories).

## Project Objectives
1. **Improve Cash Flow Visibility:** Provide real-time dashboards to track outstanding receivables and expected cash inflows.
2. **Reduce Days Sales Outstanding (DSO):** Identify bottlenecks to accelerate the invoice-to-cash cycle.
3. **Identify Payment Risk Contractors:** Segment clients into risk tiers based on historical payment performance and aging trends.
4. **Prioritize Collection Efforts:** Direct the credit control team to focus on the highest-value, highest-risk outstanding invoices.
5. **Forecast Liquidity Constraints:** Predict potential cash flow shortages based on projected payment delays.
6. **Improve Working Capital Management:** Reduce the gap between outgoing payroll costs and incoming revenue.

## Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)
The success of this analytics engagement will be measured by tracking the following KPIs:
- **Total Outstanding Receivables:** The absolute dollar amount of unpaid invoices.
- **Overdue Receivables:** The total amount of invoices past their due date.
- **Days Sales Outstanding (DSO):** The average number of days it takes to collect payment after a sale has been made.
- **Collection Effectiveness Index (CEI):** The percentage of receivables collected within a given period compared to what was available for collection.
- **Pareto Ratio (80/20):** Identifying if 80% of overdue receivables are concentrated in 20% of contractors.
- **Contractor Risk Score:** A composite score calculating the likelihood of a client defaulting or delaying payment severely.

## Success Metrics
- A clear, interactive Power BI dashboard delivered to the CFO.
- Fully normalized SQL data architecture supporting real-time querying.
- Actionable insights resulting in a targeted collection strategy that aims to reduce DSO by 15-20 days within the first quarter of implementation.
